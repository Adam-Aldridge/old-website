Spell Train

Learning to Spell

Spelling has never been one of my strong points, so to remedy that somewhat I came up with the concept of a programme that can teach you.
The idea is simple: take a list of words that are difficult to spell and feed them to the user in batches. Each "level" of 5 words can only be passed when every one of them has been spelt correctly a sufficient number of times. If a word is spelt right on the first try, it is passed immediately; whereas if not, the word is kept in the "bag" and must be spelt a few times in a row to be moved on from (the times required to pass is based on the number of incorrect spellings with a cap of 3). But where does the list of troublesome words come from? I came up with the idea of writing my masters thesis in a non-spellchecked text editor, then using a separate editor with spellcheck enabled, harvesting all of the red-underlined words as candidates for improvement. You, as I am, will likely be amused at some of the painfully basic words that I have managed to miss spell! The next step, I think, will be to create some mechanism that will sift through the ever-growing list of words to separate the actually misconceptualised (that was one) spellings from the chaff of words that were just reckless typos.
